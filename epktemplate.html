Biography

Long
Form Bio

Irma Linda Ruiz has been standing in front of crowds her entire adult life. For nearly two decades, she did it as a classroom teacher in Del Rio, Texas; managing rooms full of teenagers, navigating impossible parent-teacher conferences, and learning, show by show, that the best way to hold an audience's attention is to tell them the truth and make it funny. She retired from teaching in [Xyz]. The crowds didn't go anywhere.

A self-described former wannabe chola turned educator turned comedian, Ruiz brings a sharp perspective to the stage, the kind only earned through a real life of wild experinces. Her comedy draws from the full stack: motherhood, marriage, family dysfunction, the chaos of Latina identity, and [XYZ] years of stories from inside the American classroom that her former administrators would probably prefer she keep to herself.

Since stepping into stand-up full time, Ruiz has built one of the most active comedy careers in South Texas. She has performed at clubs, casinos, and festivals across the country, with regular appearances in Los Angeles and New York City, and international performances in Mexico and the United Kingdom. Whether she's headlining a theater in San Antonio or working a room in London, the reaction is the same: full rooms and loud laughs.

Ruiz is also the founder of MindTwistComedy, a San Antonio-based production company that has become one of the region's most prolific comedy operations. Through MindTwistComedy, she has produced original shows, developed emerging talent, and collaborated with hundreds of comedians across Texas and beyond. The company's flagship productions include Teachers Gone Bad, a standup showcase rooted in the absurdity of American education; Lotería Comedy, an interactive comedy showcase built around the classic Mexican bingo game; and Chingona Comedy, a Latina-centric show that is loud, irreverent, and proudly unconcerned with making a point.

Ruiz's material runs PG-13 to R depending on the room and the night, but the through-line is always the same: she tells you exactly what she thinks, she finds the joke in the parts of life that aren't supposed to be funny, and she leaves the audience feeling like they just spent an hour with someone who has figured a few things out. She has been banned from five Facebook Mom groups and removed from every Nextdoor she has ever joined.
